Dr Sonika Sethi

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of English at S D College, Ambala Cantt since 2008 and have specialized in Postcolonial Literature and Historical Fiction. I have a Gold Medal in M.A. English from Punjabi University, Patiala (Punjab) and am an alumna of Our Lady of Fatima Convent School, Patiala.

I completed my Doctorate in 2011 on the topic “A Comparative Study of Historicity and Indianness in Postcolonial Novels”. I am also the Executive Editor of Rhyvers Beat, a monthly, theme-based, literary magazine in English available both online and in print.

AUTHOR

I am passionate about writing. I have authored four books so far and edited three.

COLUMNIST

I contribute regular columns to national dailies- Hindustan Times and The Tribune. I have a weekly column 'Spellbound' by Sonika on rhyvers.com.

RESOURCE PERSON

ROHINTON MISTRY'S FICTION: A POSTMODERN APPROACH

When the Parsees embarked upon a journey, fearing execution, from the mighty and ancient empire of Persia little did they realize that the journey would lead them to the shores of a little known town of Sanjan in Surat in 717 A.D. What they also failed to anticipate was that one day this potent race would be standing on the verge of extinction. The journey has been a long one marred with issues concerning culture, religion, social set up and questions pertaining to identity, history, belongingness, etc. A lot was left behind, a lot was lost enroute and whatever is left needs to be preserved and conserved with utmost concern. Does the microcosm to which the Parsee characters of Rohinton Mistry's Fiction belong, capable enough to represent entire Parsee population and their concerns? Is the approach adopted by the writer holistic or constricted? Will the community regain its pristine glory or will it be consigned to the annals of history? Some of these enigmatic problems the reader should expect to come across as he/she gets on board the expedition to unravel the orphic world of Rohinton Mistry's Fiction.

OF GULMOHARS AND KANERS AND OTHER STORIES

Of Gulmohars and Kaners is the first work of fiction by the author which paints on a canvas, the geographical boundaries that extend from the Indian subcontinent to Europe to Greece and to Turkey. These pan global settings subtly convey the idea that while human emotions cannot be fathomed and scanned even by the most powerful equipment, it’s only the creative writer who can get under the skin of the characters and lay bare what goes on in a single human heart.

FROM THE SIDEWALKS OF LIFE

From the Sidewalks of Life is a collection of 80 articles published in daily newspapers, primarily, Hindustan Times. The writings cover a wide variety of themes including personal and professional experiences of the author, observations made by a passer-by on the crossroads of life, and opinions on the complexity of human relationships. These articles also cover the author’s encounters with the Two-Faced Janus, as choices crop up that resonate with the lives of many others. The language is lucid and simple, dotted with literary and erudite quotes and spiced up with a dash of humour and wit. A heightened sense of empathy and egalitarianism is also the highpoint of some of the articles.
